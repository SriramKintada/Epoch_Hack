The Problem

Most programs end with a presentation.
We end with revenue.

India does not have a talent problem. It does not have an ideas problem. What it has is an execution problem — too many brilliant engineers and researchers stuck in planning mode, waiting for the perfect conditions that never arrive.

Conventional accelerators offer months of workshops, culminating in a demo day where founders pitch to mismatched investors. Most leave with nothing but a polished slide deck.

FATHOM is built on a different principle: intense, focused execution over four weekends, with every milestone tied directly to market validation and revenue.

How It Works

Not a demo day.
A market event.

Most accelerators end with founders pitching to a room of investors who are not matched to their domain. The result is misaligned conversations and, for most founders, no meaningful outcome. A single exit path: investment, or nothing.

FATHOM's final event brings together two groups:

Investors

Pre-selected to match the specific domains of the startups in the cohort. No misaligned conversations.

Acquirers

Companies and domain players who may want to hire, acquire, or partner with the teams being built.

Four outcome pathways. Not one.

The Competitive Dynamic

When investors and acquirers are both present, something significant happens. If an acquirer signals interest in purchasing a startup, investors recognise the underlying value — and may choose to compete by offering capital instead, encouraging the founders to scale before any acquisition. This creates a market-driven environment where founders hold leverage, and the likelihood of better terms — through either investment or acquisition — increases substantially.
